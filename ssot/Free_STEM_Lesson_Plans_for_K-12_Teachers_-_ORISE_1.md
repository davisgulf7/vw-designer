# STEM Lesson Plans for K-12 Teachers

Developing a lifelong appreciation of STEM starts in the K-12 classroom, where students are challenged and encouraged to explore the wonders of scientific learning. ORISE is committed to providing relevant STEM lesson plans, educational materials, and resources to assist teachers in engaging students in STEM subjects no matter where they are in their academic career.

By challenging and encouraging students to explore and engage with STEM subjects, these lesson plans help develop critical thinking, problem-solving skills, and creativity. They also make STEM subjects accessible and enjoyable, helping students to see the relevance of these disciplines in everyday life and their potential for solving real-world problems. Moreover, well-structured STEM lesson plans support teachers in delivering effective and engaging instruction, ensuring that students of all ages can benefit from high-quality STEM education.

## K-12 STEM lesson plans and activities

Are you ready to ignite a passion for STEM in your students and equip them with the skills they need for the future. Check out our extensive library of free PDF lesson plans, STEM resources, and engaging classroom activities designed specifically for K-12 educators. Whether you're introducing elementary students to the wonders of science or challenging high schoolers with advanced engineering concepts, our resources are tailored to help your students thrive in STEM education.

Enrich your curriculum and inspire the next generation of innovators. Download hundreds of free STEM lesson plans and transform your classroom into a dynamic learning environment where every student can succeed in STEM.

Featured Activity: [AI & Us: A Critical Exploration](https://orise.orau.gov/k12/documents/lesson-plans/ai--us_-a-critical-exploration-lesson-plan.pdf) (.PDF, 328 KB)

In this lesson, students will critically explore the capabilities and limitations of artificial intelligence tools. Through hands-on activities, they will compare generalist and specialist AIs, evaluate their outputs, and engage in discussions about ethics, misinformation, and the future of AI. The lesson emphasizes digital literacy, critical thinking, and responsible technology use.
