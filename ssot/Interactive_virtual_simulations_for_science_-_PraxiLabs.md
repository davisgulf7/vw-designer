## **What are virtual simulations?**

Virtual simulations are digital and computer-based environments that simulate various real-world environments and scenarios.   These simulations enable users to interact with simulated objects as if they were in a physical setting, just imagine that you can perform your chemistry experiments virtually without the need to go to the real chemistry laboratory!   These simulations are used for several purposes, such as enhancing learning outcomes, improving practicing skills, understanding concepts in several fields:

Education.

Science learning.

Healthcare.

Games.

Training.

Engineering.

### **Nitration of Methyl benzoate**

In this experiment. You are going to learn how to run synthetic chemical reactions and learn how to prepare nitro methyl benzoate through the electrophilic nitration reaction of methyl benzoate using concentrated nitric acid in presence of concentrated sulfuric acid as a catalyzing agent.  By the end of the [Nitration of Methyl benzoate](https://praxilabs.com/en/3d-simulations/nitration-of-methyle-benzoate)** experiment, students will be able to:**

Become proficient at running organic chemical reactions.

Learn the basics of organic synthesis procedures.

Understand the mechanism of electrophilic nitration reactions for aromatic rings.

Learn the function of Electrophilic substitution reactions.

Get trained on synthetic organic chemistry techniques and skills.

### **Linear Motion on Air Track**

In this experiment you are going to learn how to analyze the motion of an object for a certain distance on a frictionless inclined surface. The purpose of this experiment is to study how the acceleration of a cart sliding on a frictionless inclined plane depends on the ramp angle, and to calculate the acceleration force.  **By the end of the ****linear motion **simulation**, students will be able to:**Analyze the motion of an object down a frictionless inclined surface.

 Study the linear motion equations.

 Set up an experiment to determine the acceleration due to gravity and the angle of inclination of the air track.

### **Citrate Test**

In this experiment, you are going to learn how to detect the presence of the citrase enzyme. The citrate test is used to determine the ability of microorganisms to use sodium citrate as the only source of carbon and inorganic ammonium hydrogen phosphate (NH₄H₂PO₄) as a source of nitrogen.  **Teach your students how to:**

To determine whether a bacterium can utilize citrate as its sole carbon source.

To identify the gram-negative pathogens of environmental isolates and the Enterobacteriaceae family.
