This site is managed by the [National Institute of General Medical Science (NIGMS)](https://www.nigms.nih.gov/). Please [contact us](https://nigms.nih.gov/Pages/ContactUs.aspx) with any questions or comments.

The external links from this site provide additional information on the resources. NIGMS cannot attest to the accuracy or accessibility of a nonfederal site.

Advancing Secondary Science Education through Tetrahymena (ASSET)

[ASSET](https://tetrahymenaasset.vet.cornell.edu/science-modules/) provides education materials that stimulate hands-on, inquiry-based learning of biology. Each lab module features living Tetrahymena, a single-celled protozoan that's safe and easy to grow and work with. Students cultivate samples of this organism in lab exercises to learn about topics such as chemotaxis, microevolution, [organelle growth and regeneration](https://tetrahymenaasset.vet.cornell.edu/cilia-growth-and-regeneration/) , and [effects of vaping](https://tetrahymenaasset.vet.cornell.edu/effects-of-vaping/) . Downloadable teacher guides and student activity sheets are also available.

**Grades:** Elementary through high school

**Produced by:** Cornell University ( [SEPA](https://www.nigms.nih.gov/Research/DRCB/sepa/Pages/default.aspx) funded)

[Learn More](https://tetrahymenaasset.vet.cornell.edu/science-modules/) [Share Resource](https://science.education.nih.gov/resource-materials/126) 

Alcohol and Your Brain: A Virtual Reality Experience

This immersive interactive teaches teens about alcohol's effects on five areas of the human brain. It simulates a rollercoaster ride weaving and bobbing its way through the prefrontal cortex, nucleus accumbens, amygdala, hippocampus, and cerebellum. Students can control the rail car and pause at stations to learn about these key brain regions affected by alcohol—and how the substance, in turn, affects behavior. Educators and students can access Alcohol and Your Brain in two ways: 1.) a [free virtual reality (VR)](https://www.oculus.com/experiences/quest/6123858984326551/) version for those ages 13+ with access to Quest, Quest 2, or Meta Quest Pro VR headsets, or 2.) a free [video](https://youtu.be/wHmf-uxOcLc) or [audio-described](https://youtu.be/O39VpkeeZAk) version.

**Grades:** Middle and high school

**Produced by:** [National Institutes on Alcohol Abuse and Alcoholism](https://www.niaaa.nih.gov/)

[Learn More](https://www.niaaa.nih.gov/alcohol-and-your-brain-virtual-reality-experience-0) [Share Resource](https://science.education.nih.gov/resource-materials/1)

Climate Change and Human Health Lesson Plans

These [climate and health](https://www.niehs.nih.gov/health/scied/teachers/cchh/) learning modules are for a variety of student audiences to explore the health impacts of climate change both in the United States and globally. Materials are suitable to teach high school courses on earth, life, and environmental sciences; history; geography; health care; or social studies. The lesson plans challenge students to consider the complex interactions between environmental health and human health and encourages them to improve their communities and design interventions to enhance climate resilience. Students also learn more about disease prevention, such as how to eliminate breeding sites for mosquitoes and how vectors—and the health risks they present in their community—may change as the climate does. Some lessons are also available in [Spanish](https://www.niehs.nih.gov/health/scied/teachers/educacion/index.cfm).

**Grades:** High school

**Produced by:** [National Institute of Environmental and Health Sciences](https://www.niehs.nih.gov/)

[Learn More](https://www.niehs.nih.gov/health/scied/teachers/cchh/) [Share Resource](https://science.education.nih.gov/resource-materials/13)

Health In Our Hands Curriculum

Health in Our Hands teaches students to investigate critical community health concerns and use real-world contexts to appreciate the importance of both genetic and environmental factors in their risk for disease. The [middle school curriculum](https://hioh.education/middle-school) consists of two units that focus on gene-environment interactions and natural selection through two subjects: Type 2 diabetes and substance use disorder. The [high school curriculum](https://hioh.education/high-school) consists of two units that focus on concepts in molecular genetics, genomics, and evolution through exploring variation in monkeyflowers and in skin color, among other topics related to human health.

**Grades:** Middle and high school

**Produced by:** [Create for STEM Institute at Michigan State University](https://create4stem.msu.edu/) in collaboration with the Health in Our Hands-Flint/Genesee Partnership ( [SEPA](https://www.nigms.nih.gov/Research/DRCB/sepa/Pages/default.aspx) funded)

What Is CRISPR?

This [Biomedical Beat blog post](https://biobeat.nigms.nih.gov/2024/10/what-is-crispr/) explores the fascinating gene-editing tool called CRISPR. It details how in the 1980s, researchers discovered odd, repeating sequences in the genomes of certain bacterial species, later named CRISPR sequences.
