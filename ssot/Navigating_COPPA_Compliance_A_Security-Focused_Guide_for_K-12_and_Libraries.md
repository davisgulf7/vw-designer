# Navigating COPPA Compliance: A Security-Focused Guide for K-12 and Libraries

1 min read

[Mary Schlegelmilch](https://blogs.cisco.com/industries/navigating-coppa-compliance-a-security-focused-guide-for-k-12-and-libraries#respond)

The Children’s Online Privacy Protection Act (COPPA) is a federal law designed to protect the online privacy of children under 13. For schools and libraries, complying with COPPA is essential—not only to meet legal obligations but also to safeguard the privacy and security of young users in today’s digital world.

## Understanding COPPA’s Basics

COPPA requires websites and online services to secure verifiable parental consent before collecting, using, or sharing personal information from children under 13. This includes names, addresses, email addresses, and persistent identifiers (e.g., cookies). The law applies to any service targeting children or knowingly collecting their data.

## Key Security Practices for COPPA Compliance

Educational institutions and libraries must prioritize strong security measures to protect children’s personal data. Critical steps include:

Data Minimization: Collect only what’s necessary for the stated purpose.

Access Controls: Restrict data access to authorized personnel.

Encryption: Protect data in transit and at rest with strong encryption methods.

Regular Security Assessments: Identify and address vulnerabilities.

Vendor Management: Ensure third-party vendors comply with COPPA standards.

## Practical Steps for Compliance

To meet COPPA requirements, follow these actionable steps:

Review Privacy Policies: Clearly outline how children’s data is collected, used, and protected. Make your policies accessible and easy to understand.

Implement Parental Consent Mechanisms: Use FTC-approved methods such as email verification or physical mail to obtain verifiable parental consent.

Provide Staff Training: Train employees on COPPA requirements and best practices for securing sensitive data.

Prepare an Incident Response Plan: Develop a structured plan for handling data breaches or unauthorized access.

Monitor Systems Continuously: Regularly check for security threats to maintain compliance and protect user data.
